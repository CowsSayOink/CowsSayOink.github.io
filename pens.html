From left to right, we have: All the nibs are either fine or extra-fine, as my handwriting is quite compact. I wish I could write with other nib sizes and play with line variation and sheen, but it would drive me crazy having my e's and l's fill up.

Story behind the pens:

Kaweco

I use a Kaweco brass (eco brass with no lead) sport to write down notes and for my assignments on a daily basis. I purchased the instrument right after my vector calculus midterm in my first year of undergrad. Since that day, it has never left me when I head down to campus.

Diplomat Aero

The story behind this one is quite lengthy... My 4th semester at McGill was quite hectic, due to weekly assignments for many of my courses. So after finals, I decided I would get something special. I wanted something that was different, so not the classic black cigar-shaped pen, and I ended up choosing the Pelikan M1000.

Pilot custom 823

This was the first high-end pen I got. It looks amazing and I had only heard great things about it, so when I saw it on discount on Pensachi, it was an immediate buy. It writes amazingly, and it is always fun to write letters with.

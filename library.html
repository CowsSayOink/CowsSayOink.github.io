2025 Reads

Buddy Reads

These are books the Lilly and Felix read together throughout the year!

Project Hail Mary

This sci-fi novel was full of science and frienship. We really enjoyed the science concepts behind the discoveries and the problem solving involved in research parts of the plot. That being said, we found the physics was explained too many times. He explained gravity at least 5 times and kept using Excel spreadsheets for calculations ranging from simple math to orbital mechanics. This book could have been 100 pages shorter with all of the unnecessary repetition.

Into Thin Air

Our first alpine non-fiction. The memoir follows Jon Krakauer's journey summiting Everest and the disaster that followed. Jon introduces the numerous teams and people on the mountain that season. It was bittersweet to learn about the individuals only to then read about their last moments. The survival half of the story is intense as the author deals with guilt and grief. The ethical dilemmas about survival in mountaineering were interesting to discuss together while reading the novel.

A Wizard's Guide to Defensize Baking

This novel features a young magician with powers related to baking. The story has a very creative magic system with a unique coming-of-age plot. We loved the baking elements of the book! Literally everytime we picked it up we wanted to bake afterwards. It surprised us that the author doesn't bake much, but was able to make a book completely centred around it. We would highly recommend this novel if you are looking for a cozy middle grade.

Murder on the Orient Express

We could not put this novel down! It follows Poirot as he tries to solve a murder of a passenger while isolated on the Orient Express. We enjoyed the writing style and the characters were both engaging and complex. We didn't know who to believe and were surprised by the ending! That being said we were dissapointed with who committed the crime. Overall we would definitely recommend!

Le Petit Prince

Unexpected gem! It is a middle grade novel about a prince who leaves his home asteroid to explore other planets - ultimately arriving on Earth. It features friendship, and childhood imagination through lyrical and humorous prose. We are really happy people of all ages can enjoy this novel.

Atonement

This historical fiction follows the Tallis family through several stages of the youngest sibling's life. The writing in this book was interesting. In some ways it felt silly that a 13 year old had an inner monologue that was more advanced than ours, but it was nicely written. The novel felt like 3 losely connected plot lines. One of the plots centered on WWII and felt unnecessary for the narrative. That being said the ending was satisfying. We later watched and enjoyed the 2007 movie.

Carmilla

Carmilla is an early vampire novel (26 years earlier than Dracula) that helped define the gothic genre! This novel is short and written origianlly in serial so it was quick and concise. The novel features a gothic romance between two female characters - one of which is a vampire!

Sunrise on the Reaping

A new exciting addition to the Hunger Games world! It was great to be back in this dystopian world again. There was a lot of nostalgia, but because of the original novels, there was never any true suspense while reading the book. This also meant that there wasn't the same magic to the world building or political commentary. Looking forward to watching the movie in 2026!

Frankenstein

Frakenstein - the first science-fiction needs no introduction! We enjoyed the story and the twists it takes. We enjoyed how the plotline takes us to locations that we have recently been to while in Europe (primarly Swiss Alps!). We really appreciated how the author encorporates nature and the outdoors into the character development and plot. The dialogue between Frankenstein and his monster is dramatic and fun to read - although funny since the monster only just learned to speak French.

Little Eyes

This is a translated dystopian novel that could be considered a thought experiment to discuss remote surveillance technology. The book features small chapters the delve into the lives of different individuals using this technology. Overall, we enjoyed the writing and pacing, but it felt like the novel did not introduce anything new to the discussion. We were left wanting a more in depth analysis of the themes the world introduces, and how it connects to our current society.

Blood Over Bright Haven

This fantasy novel features a female mage trying to work her way up in a male-dominated institution. The magic system is similar to coding which we found unique and engaging. From the first page we were hooked and wanted to learn more. The ending was complete and satisfying for us but we felt that it could have been shorter. Although initially engaging, the themes related to the patriarchical setting were repetitive by the end.

Tress of the Emerald Sea

This cozy fantasy follows Tress as she travels across various seas to save her love interest. The worldbuilding is captivating and creative. The characters have a deep and heartwarming bond with each other. The writing style is lighthearted and humourous. Felix enjoyed this tone but Lilly found it hard to connect with the characters. We are curious to read more works by Brandon Sanderson!

The Left Hand of Darkness

The author's introduction to the novel was very insightful and made us eager to delve into the story. The story itself features our protagonist on a diplomatic mission to connect with the people on a remote planet. The planet itself is very interesting. The environment is hostile and the people are non-gendered. Both of these aspects lead to an imaginative society (both technologically and socially). The second half of the novel makes survival in this environment a key part of the plot. We found this very captivating.
